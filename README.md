**Business Problem:**  
Hospitals struggle to accurately identify patients who are at high risk of developing heart disease during routine checkups. Traditional screening methods often fail to capture complex interactions between clinical featuresâ€”such as blood pressure, cholesterol, ECG abnormalities, chest pain type, and exercise-induced symptomsâ€”leading to misclassification of high-risk and low-risk patients. This results in delayed diagnoses, unnecessary testing, and inefficient use of medical resources. The organization wants to build a data-driven risk-prediction system that can classify whether a patient is likely to have heart disease based on their demographic, physiological, and clinical measurements.

**Your Role:**  
You have been brought on as a **Healthcare Data Scientist** to design an end-to-end predictive solution that estimates heart-disease risk from patient attributes. You will explore the dataset, engineer features, build ML or DL models, evaluate their performance, and ultimately deliver a deployed application that supports clinicians in making earlier and more accurate decisions during routine cardiac assessments.

## ğŸ“Š Dataset

This project uses the well-known Cleveland Heart Disease dataset, which includes 14 important clinical attributes such as age, sex, chest pain type, resting blood pressure, cholesterol, ECG results, max heart rate, exercise-induced angina, ST depression, and more.

Participants will use these variables to develop predictive models that classify the likelihood of heart disease.
